About Mid-term Examiniation

General Information

  • Mid-term examination is on Oct 21 (Monday) 8pm to 10pm
  • Venue: LTC and LTJ
  • Duration of the exam will be 2 hours.
  • Please arrive in time.
    • If you arrive late, you won't get any extra time.
    • There will be no make-up exam - make sure to come in order to get marks for the exam.
    • If you arrive after 8:30pm you will not be able to take the exam and will receive zero marks.
  • Details on the seating arrangement will be posted on the doors
  • The midterm is worth 30% of the course.
  • The exam is an open notes exam. You may bring any material that is not in electronic form.
    • All the notes and lab pages we have looked at so far have been released in the course website.
    • No solution will be released for labs, you are required to keep your own solutions. If necessary, you can print and bring your lab works to the exam.
  • Please remember to bring
    • Your HKUST Student ID card
    • Pens/pencils
  • No electronic devices are allowed during the exam. i.e. Computers, PDAs, calculators and mobile phones, etc. are not permitted and need to stay off and away from the desk.
  • You cannot share anything (such as books, paper) with anyone else once the exam has begun.

Scope of Mid-term Exam

  • Past Paper: Midterm 2012.
  • It can serve as a good guide. The format and level of difficulty will be similar but not necessarily the same.
  • The exam will include multiple choice questions, short questions, and some coding questions.
  • You will write the answers inside the exam script.
  • The exam could include questions about anything that has been discussed up to "do-while" Loops (not "for" loops).
  • While everything that was covered is included in the course notes, you can look more into any of the concepts in our self-learning center

Revising for the Exam

The exam is not a test of memorization, it is a test of understanding, so it is important to prepare for the exam:
  • Go through all the notes and labs and make sure you understand them.
  • Study the review exercises.
  • Download and run the example files to understand them.
  • Try and write your own code/programs to help understand them.
