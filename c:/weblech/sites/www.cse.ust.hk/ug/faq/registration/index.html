Answers

Recommended Pattern of Study

May I deviate from the recommended pattern of study?

Normally you should follow the "recommended" pattern of study for required courses. Deviations may delay your graduation due to prerequisite and course offering constraints. However, you may deviate from the elective recommendations if you wish. You need to check the UG Curriculum at the Undergraduate Studies for the course requirements of your program. The undergraduate curriculum by years is also available at the Undergraduate Curriculum Handbook.

I failed a required course/I transferred from another program. How can I follow the recommended pattern of study?

Cases like these may require deviating from the recommended pattern of study. Since a customized pattern of study is needed, the current ARR system cannot pre-register you in required courses that you take in semesters different from the recommended ones. Please feel free to discuss with your COMP x900 academic adviser if you need help in customizing your pattern of study.

Curriculum Changes

Changes have been made to our curriculum. Should I follow the old or new one?

The curriculum published in the Academic Calendar of the year you entered your current Computer Science program is the one that you should follow. However, some subsequent curriculum changes may also lead to slight changes in the existing curriculum and recommended pattern of study. Such changes are announced to all students concerned as soon as they are approved by the University. Please check the UG Curriculum at the Undergraduate Studies.

Semester Workload

Do I need approval if my number of credits exceeds the recommended one? Please note that the answer to this question is only applicable for students without receiving an academic probation or an academic warning.

In each semester, the suggested credit load is 15 credits. The ARRO allows students to take up to 18 credits. If you would like to take more than 18 credits, you MUST seek an explicit approval.

Method 1: We offer a simplified approval process subject to the following conditions:

  • If your CGA is A- (i.e., 3.7) or above, you can apply for a maximum of 22 credits.
  • If your CGA is C (i.e., 2.0) or above, you can apply for a maximum of 20 credits.

The recommended email request format for the simplified approval process is as follows:

Mail To:  (You MUST use your ITSC account. Otherwise, 
your request will not be processed)

Title: Credit Overload Request for Simplified Approval Process

(1) Name and student ID:
(2) Year of Study:
(3) Number of credits: (The number must be less than or equal to the 
    maximum credits allowed)
(4) Your CGA and TGA: (Please make sure that your CGA fulfills the 
    requirements)
(5) The reason for credit overloading

Attachment: The copy of your transcript

Method 2: If you would like to overload more credits than the conditions stated in Method 1, please discuss with your COMP x900 academic adviser and/or advising team for the approval. You should seek an advice well in advance before the course registration because your adviser may be out of town during your course registration.

I am on academic probation / academic warnings. I must take a reduced study load. How many credits should I take?

A student on academic probation / unsatisfactory academic performance is subject to a REDUCED study load as follows unless otherwise advised by the academic advisor of the student:

  • Not more than 9 credits for a student on academic probation with TGA < 1.0;
  • Not more than 12 credits for a student on academic probation with TGA ≥ 1.0; and
  • Not more than 12 credits for a student on academic warnings (but not academic probation) with TGA < 1.0; and
  • Not more than 15 credits for a student on academic warnings (but not academic probation and TGA ≥ 1.0) with (CGA + TGA) < 3.4.

No-disadvantage rule is applied to the undergraduate students admitted prior to September 2011 in the classification of academic standing. Please refer to the Revision of the Academic Regulation June 2011 for details.

Course Registration Problems

Under what circumstances are special add-quota requests granted?

From time to time, ARR, upon recommendation of departments, add 'special quota' during the add/drop period for cases without which the graduation or academic progress of students will be seriously hampered. However, as a standard practice, quotas will not be added for specific students to take their preferred ELECTIVES.

The following are some of the categories of cases that ARR has accepted upon the recommendation of course/major departments.

  • Dual program – students who have declared dual program designation.
  • Minor program – students who have declared minor program will need assistance to enroll for minor program courses to complete the program requirements.
  • Course re-take – students who need to re-take failed courses, mostly required courses.
  • Program transfer – students who have transferred between programs may have difficulties in getting into required courses of the new program.
  • Required courses – students who cannot meet the course pre-requisite requirements and failed to be pre-registered to required courses.
  • Exchange program – exchange-in students may need certain course places to fulfill degree requirements of their home institutions.
  • Medical reasons – some students may have medical problems that affect their choice of courses or class time.
  • Canceled courses/classes – students who are affected by the department's decision to cancel a course/class after their enrollment is confirmed.
  • Student project – subject to the support of course instructor and project supervisor (and sometimes major department), class quota may be added for some students who need to take a specific course which directly equip themselves for completion of final year project.

It should be emphasized that these are not rules for deviation from the waitlisting system, rather they are precedent cases that we have come across in the past few years. For more information on the course registration system, visit Course Registration of UG Students.

I have to take a required course in a different semester than that specified in my recommended pattern of study. The course has not been pre-registered for me and the class is full. What should I do?

Examples of circumstances in which students have to take a required course in a different semester than that specified in the recommended pattern of study are: failure in a previous attempt, deviation from pattern of study due to study leave/internship/exchange program/previously required to take a reduced study load.

You should submit an add request using the course registration system, leaving a short note explaining your case. Due to the physical limit of computer labs and classrooms, as well as the fact that there are fluctuations in the enrolment numbers during the add/drop period (first two weeks of class), we may have to wait till the second week of the add/drop period to add special places for such students. Please go ahead and attend the lecture/lab/tutorials. If your problem is still unresolved by the beginning of the second week of the add/drop period, please send an email to our Executive officer Ms. Eva Tam at .

One of my pre-registered required courses has time conflict with another course that I would like to take. I'd like to swap to another section, but that section is full. Can the department help me?

If the time conflict is with another required course that has not been pre-registered for you (e.g., honors-track courses, courses that you have failed before), please first submit an add/swap request using the course registration system, and then send an email to Ms. Brenda Tam at specifying the courses you have timetable conflict and which lecture/tutorial/lab section(s) you can attend. Please inform us as soon as possible since such requests require us to liaise with other department (e.g., LANG, MATH) and the ARRO.

As a standard practice, if the time conflict is with an elective course that you wish to take, the department CANNOT help you to swap lecture/tutorial/lab section. The ARR have very strict rules on additions of special quota. Please submit a swap request using the course registration system. If the swapping request is unsuccessful, you simply have to try to take that elective in another semester.

I have problems in registering MATH2411 (Applied Statistics). May I register another course for substitution?

If you are not able to register MATH 2411, you may consider registering IELM 2510 (Engineering Probability and Statistics) or ISOM 2500 (Business Statistics). You need to fill in GR-14 form to apply using the IELM 2510 or ISOM 2500 to satisfy the requirements of MATH 2411. Although department is happy to support the substitution, your application is subject to School level approval. In order words, you will still need to take MATH 2411 if School rejects your application.

Course Grades & Grade Averages

After passing a course, can I retake it to get a better grade?

No. The University does not allow students to repeat a passed course.

What is the difference between CGA and GGA? How are they calculated?

The CGA (cumulative grade average) is the average weighted grade obtained from all courses you have taken at HKUST. In general, the GGA (graduation grade average) is calculated from the courses that are presented for the award of a degree. Please refer to the Academic Regulations for details.

For CSE students, by default, the grades of all courses are included in your GGA calculation. However, you can choose to exclude surplus courses if you so wish. At the end of each semester, the department will ask those students who expect to graduate that semester to inform the department if they wish to exclude any surplus courses from the GGA calculation.

Note that surplus COMP courses taken during undergraduate study may be used to partially fulfill the course requirement of our MPhil or PhD program if you continue to pursue postgraduate study at HKUST. In that case, these surplus courses must be excluded from the GGA calculation.

In computing the GGA, courses taken in the first year of study of a three-year degree are given a half-weight. Students who enter the University with credit transfer of 10 credits or more are not eligible for this half-weight concession.

The GGA is used to determine the classification of honors in graduation. Please refer to the Academic Regulations for details.

Do my failed courses count towards my GGA?

Failed courses will not be automatically excluded from students' GGA. You have to apply for the exclusion. Exclusion of graded courses is subject to the following conditions:

  1. No course required for the award of the student's degree may be excluded;
  2. No more than 9 credits may be excluded. Please note the 9 credits mentioned above includes those failed courses.

Reference: Section 12.2, Graduation Grade Average (GGA), of the Academic Regulations.

Elective Courses

May I take a COMP course to satisfy the ENGG elective?

No, the ENGG elective must be a non-CS engineering course. Most ELEC courses can be counted as ENGG, but some CS-related ones cannot. Please refer to the quick reference.

What are the GEE and Common Core requirements? Which courses can and cannot be used as GE electives or Common Core?

Our curriculum aims at providing you with a well-balanced education. As such, you need to meet the general education requirement as specified by our Undergraduate Core Education Office. The Office publishes an updated list of designated education and common core courses from which you can choose to satisfy the GEE/Common Core requirement of a specific category. The course list may vary from year to year. Note that all courses that are excluded by a COMP or required course in the course catalog cannot be used to satisfy GEE requirements. In addition, ISOM 2030, ISOM 2500 and ISOM 3440 cannot be used to satisfy GEE or common core requirements. Please be also reminded that you are not allowed to take any courses that are excluded by a course that you have passed.

May I use GNED courses as electives?

All GNED courses can be counted as FREE electives.

May I use the surplus credits of core/required COMP courses as well as those of COMP, SB&M and GE electives to fulfill the credits of FREE elective requirement?

Yes, the surplus credits of core/required COMP courses as well as those of COMP electives, ENGG, SB&M, or GE electives can be used to fulfill the credits of FREE electives.

Note that surplus credits from COMP required courses (e.g., COMP 151H, COMP 3711H) cannot be used to fulfill COMP elective requirement. Also note that the COMP elective requirement is an "AND" clause, e.g., minimum 5 courses AND minimum 15 credits. For graduation checking, the ARRO separately checks whether students have fulfilled the required course requirements and the elective course requirements.

Which ISOM courses can be used as SB&M electives?

ISOM courses that are excluded by a COMP course in the course catalog cannot be counted as SB&M because they are IT-related courses rather than SB&M courses.

Which courses cannot be used as FREE electives?

Some courses cannot be taken as free electives if they are excluded by a COMP course or a required course in the curriculum of your registered program. See the quick reference.
