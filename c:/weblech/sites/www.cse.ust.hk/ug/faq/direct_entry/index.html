Credit Transfer & Expected Years of Study

All credit transfer applications must be submitted no later than one month after the commencement of the first semester of study following admission to HKUST. Late application will not be considered. For direct entry students who are admitted with a higher diploma or an associate degree, the department will fill the GR-14 form (Application for Credit Transfer/Deviation from Curriculum form) on behalf of the students based on the respective articulation plan. The ARR will inform the student the outcome of the credit transfer application in mid September.

Depending on the number of credit transfer finally approved, a direct entry student may be expected to complete the study in less than three years. Please refer to the GR-14 form returned to you to find out which semester you are expected to graduate. In the event that your graduation is delayed beyond the expected semester of graduation, you must apply for extension of period of study, which is subject to the approval of the COMP UG Coordinator and the Associate Dean of Engineering.

All transfer credits are omitted from the calculation of SGA, CGA and GGA. In computing the GGA, courses taken by students in their first year of study are normally given half weight. However, for those who are granted credit transfer on entry of 10 or more credits, all courses taken at UST will receive a weight of 1.0 in the calculation of their GGA. The weighting determined on entry will not be adjusted subsequently. Details are available in item UG 60, 3.2.3 (UG Student Progress Review, in pdf) of the Academic Programs Manual (An ITSC account is required for both login).

Pre-registration of Core/Required Courses

At the beginning of each semester, the Department will inform the ARRO to pre-register all the core/required courses specified in your recommended study patterns. Dropping these courses in the specified semester will require approval of the COMP UG Coordinator.

Promotion Requirement & Schedule

Direct-entry students may be promoted to a higher Year status before the completion of a full year of study. The promotion requirements are as follows:

  • To be promoted to Year 2 status, you need to have accumulated at least 29 credits (including the transfer credits);
  • To be promoted to Year 3 status, you need to have accumulated at least 65 credits (including the transfer credits).

For direct-entry students with an Associate Degree/Higher Diploma admitted with Year 1 status, the Department will use the following promotion schedule:

  • In early December of first year of study, the Department will inform ARRO to promote the students admitted that year to Year 2 status;
  • The ARRO system will automatically advance these students to Year 3 status effective Fall semester of second year of study.

The advantages of the early promotion are:

  • Students with a higher-year status will get higher priority random numbers for course registration purpose.
  • All students with Year-2 status in the Spring semester will be automatically eligible to select FYP using the FYP matching system.

A disadvantage of the early promotion is:

  • You may not be able to take courses that are meant for first and second year students only (e.g., SOSC 003, 111, 125, 163, 192, 193, 195).

At the beginning of each promotion exercise, the Department will send an email to inform all qualified students. Please let us know by the specified deadline if you don't want to be promoted. In that case, the student will only be promoted automatically at the beginning of each Fall semester. The department will not request ARRO to promote this student unless the student specifically submits a request.

Advice on taking SOSC courses

Some SOSC courses are meant for first and second year students only (e.g., SOSC 003, 125, 163, 192, 193, 195) or require third year students to seek instructor's approval (e.g., SOSC 111). If you are interested in taking these courses, you are advised to do so in the first semester of study, before promotion to Year-2 status. You might also want to consider retaining Year-1 status until the end of your first year of study.

LANG Course Arrangements

  • If LANG 106 has been successfully transferred for you, then simply follow the study pattern. You only need to take LANG 206 in the first year of study and LANG 306 in the second year.
  • If LANG 106 is not transferred for you, then you should take LANG 106 and LANG 206 concurrently in the first year of study and take LANG 306 in the second year.

*Note: LANG 106, LANG 206 and LANG 306 will be pre-registered for you.

FYP Arrangements

For students who are expected to graduate in 2 years OR 2.5 years

  • Select an FYP project via the online FYP management system at the end of the Spring semester of the first year of study. COMP397 will be pre-registered for you in the Fall semester of the second year of study, and COMP398 will be pre-registered in the following Spring semester.

Industrial Training Arrangements

  • Those admitted with Year 1 status should take
    • 1 module in the Winter semester of first year of study, and
    • 2 modules in the Summer semester right after first year of study
  • Those admitted with Year 2 status should take
    • 3 modules in the Winter semester of first year of study
  • The three modules are:
    • USTM13 Managing & Maintaining a Windows Server Environment
    • USTM16 Linux Systems Administration
    • USTM17 Linux Network Administration
