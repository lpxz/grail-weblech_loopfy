Academic Advising

Q: Where can I find the program catalog, course catalog and academic regulations for my studies?

Related information is available at Program/Course Catalog & Academic Regulations.

Q: I have learned programming in high school and I am already a competent programmer. Can I take other COMP courses besides COMP 1004 (COMP 104) in the first semester of study?

Yes, you may take COMP 2012 (COMP 152) or COMP 2021 (COMP 111), provided you have obtained grade B or above in HKALE Computer Studies, or obtained approval from the course instructor to waive COMP 1004 (COMP 104) as the pre-requisite. However, you are not recommended to take more than the normal study load.

Q: My curriculum needs me to take COMP 1004 Programming Fundmentals & Methodology, but it's not offered any more. What should I do?

You should take COMP 2011 Introduction to Object-oriented Programming instead, and apply course substitution. Please approach CSE admin office Ms. Brenda to obtain the substitution form. For those who failed COMP 1004 before and retake COMP 2011, no pre-enrollment will be arranged. If you haven't taken any of COMP 1021/1022P/1022Q, you need to apply for pre-requisite exemption via SIS before registering COMP 2011, and it's subjected to COMP 2011 course instructor's approval.

Q: COMP 2021 (COMP 111) is a 2000-level elective, can I take it in Year 1?

Unless you have strong programming background prior to joining UST, you are NOT advised to take COMP 2021 (COMP 111) in the first year of study. You should try to learn the basics of programming [e.g., COMP 1004 (COMP 104)] well in the first semester, and then focus on the three required COMP courses which involve substantial programming efforts in the second semester. It is important to build a good foundation in Year 1 in order to handle more advanced courses in Year 2 and 3.

Q: I plan to pursue graduate studies. Which electives are recommended?

You are recommended to take the 4-credit MATH courses in place of the 2-credit version [e.g., MATH 2121 (MATH 111) instead of MATH 2111 (MATH 113)]. In addition, you might want to take COMP 3721 (COMP 272), which is a required course for our PhD program. It is also advisable to take more mathematics courses (to fulfill the COMP/ELEC/MATH elective or FREE elective requirements). Some basic MATH courses relevant to CS are Calculus, Multivariable Calculus, Linear Algebra, Statistics, Ordinary Differential Equations, Partial Differential Equations, Numerical Analysis. For more advanced MATH courses, please seek advice from your academic advisor or the UG Coordinator.

Q: I am interested in taking some foundation ELEC courses, are there any recommendations?

In addition to ELEC 1100/2410 (ELEC 125/101) which is a required course for COMP/CSIE students, other foundation ELEC courses are ELEC 2200 (ELEC 151), ELEC 2100 (ELEC 211), and ELEC 3110 (ELEC 212). Digital Signal Processing is a useful fundamental course for several CS areas such as Image/Video/Speech Processing, Computer Graphics, and Computer Vision.

Q: Can I take a more advanced course to substitute a required course in my curriculum?

Yes, you can. For example, you may take MATH 2121 (MATH 111) to replace MATH 2111 (MATH 113), MATH 2023 (MATH 101) to replace MATH 2011 (MATH 100), MATH 2421 (MATH 241) to replace MATH 2411 (MATH 144), and MATH 2352 (MATH 151) to replace MATH 2351 (MATH 150). This is useful for students who plan to minor in MATH or declare CS-MATH double major. The extra credits earned can also be counted as FREE credits.

You should swap from your pre-registered required course to the 4-credit course, rather than drop the former and add the latter. The request to drop the pre-registered required course will only be approved when a seat has been successfully reserved for you to take the 4-credit version. Please inform Ms. Brenda Tam at when you secure the seat. Warning: Once you give up your reserved seat for the required course, should you decide later that the advanced course does not suit you, the department CANNOT help you to secure the seat back if the course is full. You have to queue for a place, and if failed, try to register for that course in another semester.

Please be reminded to fill the course substitution form (GR-14) available from the CS General Office prior to your graduation check.

Q: May I take SOSC 1100 before taking MATH 2411? If so, how are the credits counted?

SOSC 1100 is a more elementary statistics course than MATH 2411. It is possible for students taking SOSC 1100 before taking MATH 2411. However, the course contents in SOSC 1100 do not provide a more general education than MATH 2411, which is required by COMP/CSIE curriculum. The credits earned in SOSC cannot be counted towards Common Core/Designated General Education for 3-year study program. Students in 3-year study program may count the credits earned in SOSC 1100 towards Free electives.

Q: I would like to pursue a minor program, how can I plan for it?

You should plan well ahead if you are interested in pursuing a minor program. You should make use of the your electives (for both COMP and CSIE) to take courses that can be used to fulfil the requirements of the minor program. The ELEC/MATH electives or ENGG elective may be useful for pursuing certain minor programs such as MATH, Bioengineering, Technology Management. HSS GEE courses can be used to satisfy the requirements of Humanities/Social Science minor programs. More details on recommendations for pursuing minor programs.

Q: I may enroll in the double-major CS-MATH program in the second year. How can I prepare for this transfer in the first year?

You may follow the recommended study pattern of COMP or CSIE, but take the 4-credit MATH courses instead of the 2-credit ones required by COMP/CSIE. In addition, you should try to take a MATH course in place of GEE/FREE in the first-year study pattern.

Q: I didn't do well in COMP 1004 (COMP 104) and I am worried that I cannot handle the three programming-related courses COMP 151, COMP 171, and COMP 2611 (COMP 180) in the second semester. Can I delay taking any of these required courses?

COMP 1004 (COMP 104) is an important fundamental course for Computer Science students. In the unfortunate event that you have only narrowly passed COMP 1004 (COMP 104), you are strongly recommended to delay taking either COMP 2012 (COMP 152) or COMP 2611 (COMP 180). You should seek academic counseling from your academic advisor or the UG Coordinator.

Q: I wish to transfer between CSIE and COMP programs, which have different MATH required courses. Do I have to take extra MATH courses?

If you transfer from CSIE to COMP, you must add MATH 2011 (MATH 100). If you transfer from COMP to CSIE, you must add MATH 2351 (MATH 150).

Q: What if I am not able to attend final examination?

If students wish the University to take into account illness or some other extenuating circumstances that have affected their performance in an examination, or ability to attend an examination, or to complete other assessment activities, they must report the circumstances of the case in writing and provide appropriate documentation to ARRO within one week of the scheduled date of the assessment activity. Please refer to Illness or Other Circumstances Affecting Assessment for more information.

Q: Where can I find information on the credit transfer of courses taken during exchange-out periods?

Related information is available at Courses and Credit Transfer from SENG (login required).

Q: I have encountered problems in academic study. What do I need to do before meeting with my academic adviser?

If you understand that you are in unsatisfactory academic progress (UAP) or have received a warning letter from the University, you are strongly advised to complete the Academic Progress Review Form, send it to your adviser together with the supplementary information, and seek advice from your academic adviser.
